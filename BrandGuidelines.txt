3) Correct and Incorrect Logo Usage

Use the Ribo logo consistently and correctly to maintain brand clarity, legibility, and trust across all applications.

Always

Use the logo in its original proportions

Apply only approved light or dark variants

Maintain sufficient contrast between logo and background

Placement

Place the logo on clean, uncluttered backgrounds

Ensure adequate clear space around the logo

Avoid competing elements near the logo

Why it matters

Builds strong brand recognition

Preserves visual clarity

Reinforces a professional brand presence

(Correct Logo Usage, page 8) 

RIBO - Brand Guidelines

Don’t

Stretch the logo

Change the logo’s colors

Rotate or skew the logo

Place the logo on low contrast or busy backgrounds

(Incorrect Logo Usage, page 9)

6) Icon Style

Icons used within the Ribo brand should:

Follow a simple, geometric visual style

Use consistent stroke weight across all iconography

Prefer outline-style icons with rounded/subtle corners

Stay functional and minimal

Support content/usability (not decorative/illustrative)

7) Tone of Voice
Clear & Confident

Description: Clarity and assurance; expertise without arrogance

Do: direct, plain language; decisive and informative

Don’t: vague wording, filler, uncertainty

Professional but Approachable

Description: Polished and credible, friendly and easy to understand

Do: respectful conversational tone; explain simply

Don’t: cold, overly formal, or overly casual

Helpful & Human

Description: Prioritize user needs with empathy and understanding

Do: acknowledge challenges; offer practical guidance

Don’t: dismiss concerns; sound robotic

Straightforward & Concise

Description: Value users’ time; communicate efficiently

Do: get to the point; short sentences; clear structure

Don’t: over-explain; jargon without context; unnecessary detail

Tech-Forward & Reliable

Description: Innovation + trust via thoughtful, accurate communication

Do: use precise terminology when needed; ensure accuracy

Don’t: buzzwords/hype; promises you can’t support

(Tone of Voice, page 17)
